Follows the SPICE framework. Focuses on discovery questions that uncover pain before pitching the solution.

# Sales Discovery: [Company Name]

**PRE-CALL CHECKLIST**
- [ ] Research LinkedIn
- [ ] Check CRM history
- [ ] Verify person's role

**OPEN (2 mins)**
- Goal: "Verify if Glance solves [Pain Point]"
- Agenda: Discovery -> Demo -> Next Steps

**DISCOVERY**
- Situation: "Walk me through how you handle [Process] today?"
- Impact: "What happens if [Metric] doesn't improve?"
- Priority: "Why solve this now?"

**THE PITCH**
- Tie to pain: "You mentioned [Pain]. Here is how we fix it."
- Value 1: [USP]

**OBJECTIONS**
| Objection | Response |
| :--- | :--- |
| Too expensive | Focus on ROI |
| No time | Focus on speed-to-value |

**CLOSE**
- "Based on this, should we move to a pilot?"

- "Does this align with your technical requirements?"

Why a good script matters more than ever

In a remote-first world, your video presence is your professional signature. Winging it often leads to rambling, missed points, and meetings that run over time. A well-structured script keeps you concise, respectful of everyone's time, and focused on the outcome.

However, writing a script is only half the battle. If you're constantly looking down at your desk or off to a second monitor to read your notes, you break eye contact and lose your audience's trust. The dreaded "reading side-glance" makes you look disconnected and unprepared.

That's why these templates are formatted in clear, scannable Markdown. They are designed to act as visual cues rather than a teleprompter word-salad. Pair them with a transparent desktop overlay like Glance, and you can keep your notes floating right beside your webcam—maintaining perfect eye contact while never missing a beat.
